Open source packages improve thanks to people like you who kindly contribute to open source software. Your contribution to the packages we build can be as simple as raising an issue. Here is how you can do just that.

Each packages has a website with a link to report a bug or raise any relevant issue (more on this below). For example, the website of the package r2dii.match lives here and looks like this:

Figure 1: The website of every package has a link to report a bug.

The link to report a bug opens a page with two fields:

  1. A Title (like the subject of an email) to summarize your issue;
  2. A body to detail your issue. Please follow our instructions carefully to avoid emails and solve the issue quickly. We know you are busy.

Figure 2: The web page to report a bug is like an email.

Whats beyond our scope

Our scope is limited. We assume you are familiar with R. We cant teach you R but you may like our favorite resources to learn R. The issues we can address are those caused specifically by the packages we build. Report everything else at https://stackoverflow.com/ or https://community.rstudio.com/.

The most common out-of-scope problems are installation errors. Its unlikely the problem comes from our packages because we continuously check that they install correctly with multiple version of R on Mac, Windows, Linux, and several other platforms (example). What should you do then?

A tale of two R programmers

Mike and Lucy are working together and want to use the package r2dii.match. They try to install it with install.packages("r2dii.match") but they get an error.

Lucy: Bummer!

Mike: Wait; lets read the error message carefully.

The error message makes no sense. Lucy Googles it and finds some promising results. Mike tries a few hacks but nothing works.

Lucy: Its all sooooooo confusing; but there is something about versions, right? These R geeks move fast they pump new stuff every day!

Mike: What if we ask at https://community.rstudio.com/? Everyone there seems quite friendly?

Lucy: Yes, those folks are cool; but lets first update everything I mean R, RStudio, and all R packages. It cant hurt and sooner or later well have to do it anyway. Here is a nice guide.

Mike: Yeah, it never seems like a good time to update. Lets just bite the bullet.

After an hour or so they are done.

Lucy: Okay, here we go again: install.packages("r2dii.match").

Mike: Fingers crossed.

They look at each other. Then Lucy runs the code. Then it just works.

Takeaway:

Get current, people. You dont want to adopt new things on day one. But at some point, running old versions of software adds unnecessary difficulty. How to think about upgrading R and RStudio
